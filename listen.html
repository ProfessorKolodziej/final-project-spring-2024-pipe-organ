An organ stop is a family of pipes that share a name and a similar shape.

Organ Stop Descriptions

The Principal 16' is a deep supportive sound that is often played on the pedalboard with the organist's feed. It is called 16 because its pipes are twice as long as the Principal 8'.

Vox Celeste means voice of the sky. A celeste stop plays multiple pipes together to make its special sound.

Principal 8' is the classic pipe organ sound. A middle C pipe is really 8 feet tall!

Nazard is a mixture stop where multiple pipes sound together to create an illusion of sound.

The Trumpet 8' is usually a loud solo stop that sounds exactly like the brass instrument it is named after.
